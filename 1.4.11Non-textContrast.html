WCAG 2.2 - 1.4.11 Non-text Contrast

❌ Bad Example: Low Non-text Contrast

The following UI elements fail because their borders, backgrounds, or focus indicators have poor contrast against adjacent colors.

Low contrast icon - hard to distinguish
  • Button border and background blend with page color → low visibility.
  • Icon shape is barely visible against background → fails 3:1 ratio.
  • Input field border lacks sufficient contrast (hard to see outline).
  • Focus outline is too faint → keyboard users can’t see focus position.

✅ Good Example: Sufficient Non-text Contrast

These controls pass accessibility checks — they meet or exceed a 3:1 contrast ratio for non-text elements like borders and focus indicators.

High contrast icon - easily identifiable
  • Button background and border contrast well against the page.
  • Icons stand out clearly even for low-vision users.
  • Input field has dark border for easy identification.
  • Keyboard focus indicator (orange outline) is highly visible.

✅ Exception Example: Disabled Elements or Decorative Graphics

Elements that are purely decorative or disabled are not required to meet non-text contrast rules.

Explanation: This button is disabled and non-interactive, so low contrast is acceptable.
