WCAG 2.2 – 1.4.3 Contrast (Minimum)

Guideline: Text and interactive elements must have enough color contrast against their background to ensure readability.

Bad Examples (Fail Contrast Ratio)

Poor contrast: Yellow text on yellow background (fails contrast ratio)
Light gray text on light gray background – difficult for low vision users

Graphical element with low contrast between bar and background – fails WCAG 1.4.3.

Good Examples (Pass Contrast Ratio)

Normal text: Black on white – Contrast ratio 21:1 (Excellent)
White text on dark blue – Contrast ratio ~12:1 (Pass)
Large text (20pt) with 3:1 contrast still acceptable (Pass for large text)

Dark green bar on white background – good visual contrast (Pass).

Explanation of Scenarios

  • Scenario 1 – Low color contrast text: Fails if text contrast ratio < 4.5:1 (for normal text).
  • Scenario 2 – Large or bold text: Passes with ratio ≥ 3:1 (since it’s easier to read).
  • Scenario 3 – Buttons and UI elements: Must have visible text or icons with ≥ 3:1 contrast ratio.
  • Scenario 4 – Graphical elements: Important visual parts (like charts, borders, or indicators) must also meet 3:1 contrast.
  • Scenario 5 – Background images: Avoid placing text directly on images unless a solid color overlay ensures contrast.

  • WebAIM Contrast Checker
  • Contrast Ratio Tool

Note: Decorative text or disabled elements are exempt, but essential content must always meet contrast standards.
